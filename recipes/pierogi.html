Pierogi with potatoes and cottage cheese

Description

Image and the recipe taken from aniagotuje.pl - I'm not the author, nor do I claim any rights to them.

Pierogi (pol.), or Вареники (ukr.) are a Central/Eastern European version of dumplings- they are a simple dish consisting of variety of fillings wrapped in a "pocket" of dough and cooked in boiling water.

Ingredients:

  • Dough:
    • flour: 500g,
    • water: 250ml,
    • vegetable oil: 50ml (4 tablespoons),
  • Stuffing:
    • cottage cheese: 300g,
    • 1 large onion: 300g,
    • clarified butter: 2 tablespoons,
    • salt: half of a teaspoon
    • pepper: half a teaspoon

Steps

  1. Prepare the potatoes

    Peel 650-700 grams of potatoes and boil them soft in salted water. Drain the water, dry the potatoes thoroughly and mash them. You should end up with not more than 500 grams of purée. leave it to cool down, and add salt and pepper- half of a tablespoon each.

  2. Fry the onion

    Peel and dice a large onion (you can use smaller ones with total weight of about 300 grams). On a heated frying pan, put two tablespoons of butter. Add the diced onin and fry it on medium heat. It you prefer not to use butter, you can replace the butter with oil.

  3. Prepare the cottage cheese

    Take 300 grams of cottage cheese and pass it through a mincing machine or a potato press.

  4. Mix the stuffing ingredients

    Put the mashed potatoes, cottage cheese and fried onion together and mix them well.

  5. Prepare the dough

    Take a large bowl and sieve 500g of flour to it. Add half teaspoon of salt and four tablespoons of oil to it. Pour a glass of hot water to the bowl, mix the ingrediends with a spoon, and strart kneading the dough once it starts taking the consistency. The dough should be soft and elastic when you're done.

  6. Shape your dough into a ball

    Wrap the ball in cling film and let it rest for 30 minutes. Thanks to hat the dough will keep its shape during rolling.

  7. Roll the dough

    After letting the dough rest, unwrap it from the film and split it to three parts. Put two pieces aside and cover them with a cotton kitchen towel to prevent them from drying.

  8. Shape the pierogi

    Take one piece of the dough and roll it on a table or pastry board sprinkled with flour. Cut out round pieces around 8 cm in diameter. Put a tablespoon of the stuffing inside each dough piece, then fold it in half and pinch the open side closed.

    Put the prepared pierogi next to each other on a pastry board, and cover them with a slightly damp cotton kitchen towel. to prevent them from drying.

  9. Boil the pierogi

    Put each set of pierogi in boiling, salted water for 3 to 4 minutes. Take them out of water with a strainer spoon when they float to the surface.

    You can serve finished pierogi with melted butter and fried onion as garnish.
